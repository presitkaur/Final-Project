About the Project

Gaming, everyone has done it

Whether it's simply just playing Candy Crush or entering the high stakes world of e-sports, gaming has become a major part of recreation in modern day society.

As technology advances, so do the platforms that games are released on. Just within a short twenty years, the impossible has been made possible yet time and time again. At the start of the centry, the iconic Play Station 2 was the talk of the community. Now we can find ourselves immersed in a virtual world through VR headsets and 3D animation.

By looking at five gaming platforms from recent past years, this project will attempt to analyse which generated the most sales and best reviews as well as if any patterns appear in reviews for the platforms.

Platforms

The Five Platforms of Interst

The following is a list of the platforms this study will focus on:

Play Station 4

Initially released in late 2013, this console was the successor to Sony Computer Entertainment Play Station 3. Many iconic games such as The Last of Us and Fall Out 4 are available on this platform.Retailing at around the $350-$450 AUD mark, this powerful console is still widely used today. The set up consists of a console and a controller.

Play Station Vita

This handheld console was released in December 2011 by Sony Computer Entertainment. It's main rival during this time was Nintendo's 3DS. Design was inspired by the well known GameBoy Advance consoles. Some well known games to be released on this platform are Persona 4 Golden and Assassins Creed III: Liberation.

XBox One

This console and controller platform was the main compeition to the Play Station 4. Released late 2013 by Microsoft, it aimed to deliver a more refined and streamlined gaming experience than its precursor, the XBox 360. Its main advantage over Play Station was its multimedia and co-op applications. Notable games on this platform are Grand Theft Auto V and the Forza Series. Production of this console was halted some time around 2020 as Microsoft worked on releasing newer XBox consoles. Today, a second hand XBox One retails at around the $200-$300 AUD mark.

WiiU

The WiiU was the console that first started the 8th Generation of Gaming Consoles. Released in 2012, it was the first Nintendo console to support HD graphics. The design interface on the WiiU GamePad is similar to the Play Station Vita. This platfrom is home to exclusive games such as The Legend of Zelda: Twighlight Princess HD, Splatoon and Mario Party 10. Production of the Wii U ended in Janurary 2017.

PC

PC's are difficult to describe with one simple definition. Generally they consist of a keyboard, mouse/mousepad and a screen or monitor. This platform has been no stranger to the technological revolution and is highly customisable. Games generally tend to be cheaper on this platform due to licensing fees incured on consoles such as the PS4 or XBox One. The gamer is able to fully customise their PC to suit their gaming desires. This can include installing new hardware (such as a improved graphics card) or upgrading their set up. Undertale and Minecraft are just two of the well known games available on this platform.

Sales

How did These Platforms Perform in Sales of Video Games?

The Eighth Generation of Video Game Consoles

Began with the release of the WiiU in 2012.

Although it's debatable when exactly the Eighth Generation ended, it is reasonable to assume it ended between the years 2017 and 2020. This generation brought an advanced and refined gaming experience. As well as that, a lot of consoles released during this period not only fulfiled their gaming purpose but also provided other services such as Netflix or social media applications.

The bubble chart gives a simple look at the total sales for of video games for each platform. As seen, Play Station 4 has the largest bubble, indicating that video games released on this platform generated the most sales in total.

This next line graph provides a glance at each year and how the platform performed in terms of video game sales. As seen, 2015 saw a great amount of sales for the Xbox One and Play Station 4 consoles. Abrupt line starts for these consoles indicate that they were released within the year the line begins.

Many lurking variables can attribute to the trends seen in both graphs. A major one is the way in each platform is marketed. PC's, for example, can be marketed in numerous ways such as as a study tool, office tool or gaming tool. As well as that, buying a video game is just that. It does not reflect user opinion of that game on the platform. A user can buy a game but then never play it or only play half of it. This is not very useful when attempting to determine if a relationship exists for this study.

Each graph is fully interactive. Tooltips are available upon hover to reveal how much exactly was amassed in sales.
